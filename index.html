Nelson Mandela

The First Black President of The Republic of South Africa

Here's a time line of Mandela's life:

  • 1918 - Mandela was born on July 18, 1918 in Mvezo, South Africa.
  • 1942 - Vitvaterstrand University in 1942 After graduating from the law department, he started working as a lawyer. He received the title of the country's first black lawyer.
  • 1944 - Mandela made his first marriage to Evelyn Ntoko Mase in 1944
  • 1962 - In January 1962, he went abroad to seek support. He traveled to England and African countries.
  • 1962 - He was arrested in 1962 and sentenced to life imprisonment for conspiracy and sabotage to overthrow the government. Mandela served his sentence first on Robben Island and then at Pollsmoor Prison.
  • 1981 - Mandela was awarded the Bruno Kreisky Prize for Human Rights in 1981
  • 1990 - In the meantime, an international campaign was organized for his release to be approved in 1990, that is, 27 years later.
  • 1990 - Nelson Mandela has been called the world's most famous prisoner. After being imprisoned for 27 years on Robben Island (Seal Island) in South Africa , he became famous in the 1980s when the struggle against racism intensified all over the world.
  • 1993 - Mandela gained international acclaim for his anti-colonial and anti-apartheid views and won over 250 awards, including the 1993 Nobel Peace Prize , the United States Presidency Medal of Freedom, and the Soviet Order of Lenin . He is considered the "Father of the Nation" in South Africa .
  • 1994 - Mandela, who became head of the ANC after his release from prisonhe wrote his autobiography , and the establishment of an election with president FW de Klerk in 1994, in which the entire population participated and the ANC won by a large majority, led to talks to end Apartheid.
  • 2008 - Mandela was removed from the US terrorist list in 2008
  • 2013 - Mandela was hospitalized on June 8, 2013 and died on December 5, 2013.
